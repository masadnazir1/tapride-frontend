## Choosing the Right Vehicle

Northern roads can be challenging. Consider:

- **SUVs or 4x4 vehicles:** Recommended for hilly terrain, rough roads, and occasional off-road trips.
- **Sedans or small cars:** Suitable for main highways if traveling light.
- **Fuel-efficient cars:** Important for long distances where fuel stations may be sparse.

## Best Routes to Explore

1. **Islamabad → Murree → Naran → Kaghan Valley**  
   Scenic route with forests, rivers, and viewpoints.

2. **Gilgit → Hunza Valley → Karimabad → Attabad Lake**  
   Stunning mountain views and turquoise lakes.

3. **Skardu → Shigar → Deosai National Park**  
   Ideal for adventure seekers, but requires proper off-road vehicles.

**Tip:** Plan overnight stays in advance; popular tourist areas fill up quickly.

## Essential Tips for Renting a Car

1. **Book Early:** Northern Pakistan is a peak tourist region, especially during summer.
2. **Check Vehicle Condition:** Tires, brakes, AC, and engine must be in top condition.
3. **Confirm Insurance:** Comprehensive coverage is recommended for mountainous routes.
4. **Hire a Driver (Optional):** Experienced local drivers know challenging roads better.
5. **Check Fuel Availability:** Some remote areas have limited fuel stations.

## Avoid Common Mistakes

- Driving at night in mountainous areas.
- Ignoring weather updates – sudden rains or snowfall can block roads.
- Overpacking; smaller vehicles may have limited luggage space.
- Not keeping a GPS or offline maps; network coverage is weak in remote valleys.
